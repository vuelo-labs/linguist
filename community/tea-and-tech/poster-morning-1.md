# Tea & Tech — Poster (Morning 1, launch in a new town)

A4 portrait. Designed to be readable from across a parish hall noticeboard or library window. Printed on the local printer; no glossy stock needed — this should look like it belongs on the corkboard, not like an ad.

## Notes on choices

- **No mention of Claude or AI in the headline.** The word "AI" is in the body once, deliberately small. The headline sells *not waiting*, not technology. People who already know what AI is don't need the poster; people who don't will be put off by the word in 90pt.
- **"…back in school"** is a deliberate signal to anyone who left school at 14 and didn't enjoy it. This is the sentence that gives them permission to come.
- **"Bring your phone. We'll do the rest."** removes the biggest pre-attendance worry: *"do I need to bring something? do I need to know something already?"*
- **"Just turn up."** matters. A sign-up form on a poster halves attendance for this demographic. The optional phone number is for the worriers, who are also the ones most likely to come.
- **Phone & email symbols, not URLs.** A QR code on a poster aimed at people who don't know what AI is would be self-defeating. Phone number is the channel.
- **Sponsor logo at the bottom, not the top.** They get credit; they don't get the headline. This is also what makes sponsoring it feel honourable rather than commercial — and what makes the *next* sponsor easier to sign.

## Variants

**The standing poster** (weeks 2 onward in the same town): swap headline to:

> *Pop in if you don't want to wait til the weekend.*

Same layout. The "your son" version is for the launch poster only — its job is to stop someone in the corridor on the first run. After that, the gentler version ages better and doesn't sting the attendee whose son doesn't ring.

**The "bring a friend" poster** (Morning 2 onward, on the way out the door of Morning 1):

> *You came on your own. Bring someone next time.*

Smaller, A5, on the table by the tea urn. Different job — not recruitment from cold, but warmth from inside the room.

## What the booklet cover changes to

Front cover of the Morning 1 booklet should carry the standing tagline, not the launch one — the booklet lives on the kitchen table for weeks, and *"wait for your son to call"* will read sharper there than on a poster glanced at once.

Cover line:

> **Tea & Tech**
> *Pop in if you don't want to wait til the weekend.*
>
> Morning 1: Getting Claude on Your Phone

## Print spec

- A4, 200gsm matte. Not glossy — glossy reads as advertising.
- Black text on cream or off-white. Avoid pure white (glares under fluorescent hall lights, and reads colder).
- One accent colour, used sparingly: the underlines, the icons, the sponsor block. Pick a warm one — a deep red, a forest green, a mustard. Not blue (reads "council") and not purple (reads "tech startup").
- Print 20 per town for the launch — 5 for the venue, 5 for the local shop windows that will say yes (SuperValu, the post office, the credit union branch, the library, the GP waiting room), and 10 spare for the host to put up wherever they walk past in the week before.
