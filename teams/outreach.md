# Outreach Copy — v0

**Status:** Draft, 2026-05-06.
**Audience:** Heads of Engineering, VP Engineering, Heads of DevEx, Heads of People (eng-org-adjacent), CTOs at companies 50–500 engineers. First-degree LinkedIn connections first.
**Goal:** Book a 30-minute call. Optionally surface a free baseline audit at the end of the first reply.

**Rules:**
- One paragraph + one ask. Never three paragraphs.
- Name the rubric. The rubric is the differentiator; lead with it.
- Don't promise numbers. "Measured" not "30% savings."
- Don't say "AI training" — that's the commodity vendor space. Say "AI-fluency capability programme" or "team capability assessment."
- Sign with first name + role + one-line context. No corporate boilerplate.

## Reply playbook

**They reply "interested, tell me more":**
Send the [one-pager link](https://linguist.vuelolabs.com/teams/one-pager/) with one sentence. Don't write a sales paragraph. Let the page do the work.
> "One-pager here: [link]. Happy to find time if you want to scope a baseline."

**They reply "we already have AI training":**
Don't compete on training. Compete on *measurement*.
> "Most AI training is content; this is a rubric and an assessment. The training piece is optional — most orgs we talk to start with just the baseline audit because it tells them what training (if any) they actually need."

**They reply "send pricing":**
Send the [one-pager](https://linguist.vuelolabs.com/teams/one-pager/) — pricing's on it. No discount language; the rubric is the value, not the hourly rate.

**They reply "interesting but not now":**
Note timing. Add to the list to revisit in 6 weeks. Don't push.

**They go quiet after first reply:**
One follow-up at +7 days, one at +21 days, then close the thread. Don't trickle.

---

## What to NOT say

- ❌ "AI consulting" — generic, crowded
- ❌ "Boost productivity" — no buyer believes this anymore
- ❌ "Save N% on tokens" — number you can't promise
- ❌ "Disrupt your workflow" — vendor-speak
- ❌ "Just 15 minutes of your time" — patronising

---

## Tracking

In the lead sheet, log per outreach: variant used, date sent, reply date, reply category (interested/declined/silent/timing), follow-up dates, outcome. After 30 sends, look at the variant-A vs variant-C reply rate to decide what to scale.
